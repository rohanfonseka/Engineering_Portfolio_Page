Machine Control Design: Harvester

April 20 2018 - May 4 2018, Principles of Engineering

The Machine Control Design Project consisted of me and my team creating a mechanical prototype out of VEX Robotics building materials and powered by a VEX cortex that would perform a specified task. The problem we chose to solve was called the Harvester. We had to design a robot that would travel down an aisle and pick up lego pieces (representing plants to be harvested) then put them into a basket on its back. My responsibilities were to create the design of the robot, then assist in the basic components building and programming of the robot to perform the task. Given my solid coding background, my team relied on me to create an easy to read code that got the job done at max efficiency. Throughout the project, I learned skills such as design fundamentals, coding structure and VEX RobotC coding language. I developed my teamwork skills, leading many of the day to day tasks and helping out wherever I could. The skills I exemplified in this project were programming, teamwork and 3D design.

Compound Systems: D.A.F.F.

August 30 2017 - September 10 2017, Principles of Engineering

The objective of this project was to create a compound machine, using the different simple mechanisms we learned about in class, that was able to do one simple everyday task with the turn of a wheel or a push of a lever. My responsibilities as a member of the group were to assist my teammates in stability design and creativity, and some individual deliverables including brainstorming sketches and calculations for the efficiency of the final design. In addition to being the lead structural engineer, I created the idea of DAFF in one of my original brainstorming sketches. Through doing this project, I learned how to calculate the efficiency of different simple machines connected to each other as well as general methods on how to make Fischer Tech structures more stable. I proved myself to be a positive and supportive groupmate all throughout the project, helping here and there with practically all aspects of the project one way or another, and tying it all together. Some skills that I exemplified during this project were teamwork, motivation, and technical proficiency. I used my knowledge of simple machines to solve the problem.

Python: Image Manipulation

March 2017, Introduction to Computer Science and Engineering

The objective of this project was to create a python algorithm that would manipulate a given folder of image files to fullfill a specified theme. My partner and I went about this challenge by first selecting jpg file images to be put into a folder. We then attempted to come up with ideas such as turning the images black and white or distorting the color scheme. Once we found out how to manipulate a numpy array from the reference sheet we were given and some online research, it was all a matter of creativity. We ended up coming up with a patriotic design that would act as a transparent mask over the image. Our code didn't actually paste a transparent mask, but instead created one by going through each and every pixel and manipulating it through a series of algorithms to produce a tinted color of the pixel.

Scratch: Save the Princess

September 2016, Introduction to Computer Science and Engineering

The purpose of the project was to create a fully functioning game using the basic knowledge of Scratch game design block coding that we knew. My group and I decided to create a levels based game that involved a prince traveling through room after room to save his true love. The whole premise was similar to that of Mario. Each level presented its own set of challenges including the delegation of variables, the manipulation of sprites, and the verbal communication between groupmates. In the end, we created a multi-level videogame involving the use of many different variables and operators and the usage of numerous sprites.
